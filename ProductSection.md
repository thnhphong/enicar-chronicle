### 2a. THE GOLDEN ERA
Source: PDF page 1, verbatim

```
Enicar earned a reputation in the mid-20th century for producing
high-quality tool watches that combined technical reliability with
bold design at prices well below those of luxury competitors such
as Rolex and Blancpain.

Models like the Sherpa Graph, Sherpa Jet, and Sherpa Super-Dive
featured in-house movements, robust EPSA compressor cases, and
real-world testing by climbers, racers, and military divers.

Despite this engineering credibility, Enicar remained remarkably
affordable, making professional-grade Swiss watches accessible to
a wider global audience, especially in Asia, where the brand built
a strong following. This value-driven approach helped position
Enicar as a brand for doers, not just collectors.
```

---

### 2b. THE BIG PROBLEM
Source: PDF page 1 + page 3, verbatim

```
Enicar was slow to invest in quartz development compared to
Japanese and some Swiss rivals, making it vulnerable when consumer
demand shifted rapidly in the 1970s. Despite supplying
high-performance watches (e.g., Sherpa Dive, Graph), Enicar lacked
the marketing clarity and documentation to establish mainstream
professional credibility like Rolex or Omega.

After acquired by Wah Ming Hong in 1987, the brand under new
ownership did not preserve or invest in the original archives,
making it difficult to build continuity with collectors or use
historical storytelling in branding. Without clear design direction
or mechanical credibility, modern Enicar watches were largely
ignored by serious watch communities, further eroding the brand's
value in the long term.

After going bankrupt after the Quartz crisis in 1987, the brand
became a "zombie" entity under new ownership, ignoring its rich
history of exploration and racing to produce generic, mass-market
dress watches that rely on the logo's recognition rather than
horological innovation.

On today's market, the brand is often undervalued compared to its
former competitors, despite its significance in the past and the
quality of the watches. Mainly because of the lack of storytelling
and sharing of knowledge within the community.
```

### 2c. PROJECT AIM
Source: PDF page 1 + page 3, verbatim

```
PREVENT THE BRAND FROM BEING OBSOLETE
```

Why:
```
- The design can't be replicated by other brands.
- There are still past customers and a loyal fanbase.
- There are seasoned watch collectors who are open to learn
  about the brand.
- There are brands that are on the verge of becoming obsolete
  that might benefit from this project.
```

## 10. DESIGN PRINCIPLE (from PDF page 1 + page 3)

The brand's problem was:
"lack of storytelling and sharing of knowledge within the community"

The solution is:
"archive-based, human-factors storytelling"

Every design decision in this section should serve that principle.
The watch is never the hero. The story is the hero.
The watch is proof that the story happened.
